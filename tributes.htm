Tributes

Daddy, I recall in 2000 when I was leaving for the university and that was the first time, I was leaving your home. I was accompanied by my uncle Engr. Gideon Obasi. You accompanied us to the gate and shook my hands and looked into my eyes and said now you are a man. At that point, I didn’t understand what you meant but much later when I found myself in campus without you or mum around, I had to learn to be myself and stay true to the values I got from you. I realized your words and have ever appreciated you. In all my sojourn within and outside the country, I have been blessed by your examples. Whenever I met crossroads and difficulties, I have always remembered your examples in prayer. Whenever I found myself in a heated and provocative environment, your examples of peace always showed me the way out. Whenever I felt distraught about my inadequacies, your example of contentment encouraged me to hang on. I love you Dad more than words could ever express and cherish every moment I shared with you. Although distance was a barrier, we were never apart because we kept in touch in so many ways. It was a delight for my kids or my wife to often speak with you over the phone and play around you whenever they visited. I had prayed earnestly to God to keep you longer, but it pleased God to call you home at this time and I cannot question God. I am consoled because the bible tells me that you are in a better place even though I cannot see your face. I know you’re smiling down at me. I am encouraged by Revelations 14: 13 “And I heard a voice from heaven saying unto me, Write, blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth: Yea, saith the Spirit, that they may rest from their labours; and their works do follow them” Adieu Daddy, you have labored and now its time to rest in perfect peace and comfort.

Engr. Princewill Chidiebere Ahamaefule Amaefule (Deacon, Church of Christ Nyanya Abuja) For the entire family
