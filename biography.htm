Biography

Education

Prince Felix Amaefule was enrolled at the Apostolic Church School in 1952 and in 1956 he emerged tops in his Standard 6 exams obtaining his First School Living Certificate. He then proceeded to Harris Commercial Secondary School Aba from 1958 to 1962 where he obtained His Royal Society for Arts & Commerce Stage 2 (Intermediate) with Second Class in English Language.

After graduation from secondary school, Prince Felix had to seek for clerical jobs in order to sustain his ambition for further studies and in 1964 the opportunity came when he secured admission with the Gordon Allen College London England where he pursued Advanced Diploma in Accounting & Book Keeping in 1966. The political situation and resulting 30 months civil war in the country affected his desire to pursue further studies as the level of deprivation and poverty mated on the people of his region influenced him to seek employment.

Marriage and Family

They say marriage brings two families together, but in this case, the families of Amaefule Osuala and Obasi Iwuoha were already enjoying rich friendship as both fathers were good friends. So, it was not difficult that in 1967/68 when young Felix Amaefule approached his father that he wanted to get married, his father simply nudged young Felix towards the direction of his friend’s daughter, the beautiful and graceful Comfort Ozichi Obasi, considering her great qualities. They got married in 1968 and their marriage was blessed with 6 Children; five females and one male.

Prince Felix Amaefule, had experienced sad moments during his lifetime. In 2004, after 36 years of marriage, God called his wife home. Due to the bond that existed between them many thought he would not survive. In 2013, He remarried but unfortunately the marriage lasted only one year as the wife died after a brief illness. In 2016, Mr. Felix received the shocking news of the death of His first daughter who died during her fifth child birth, while her husband who was terminally ill later died after two weeks. It was devastating for him to attend the funeral of his first daughter and her husband. However, His faith in God and resilience kept him going as he adopted the four grand children left by his late daughter. Until his death Mr. Felix Amaefule, kept his immediate family and extended family in prayers and showed great fatherly example both to his Children and people around him.

Career

Prince Felix Amaefule started his working career in the mid 60s shortly after completing his secondary education. This was in order to sustain his desire for save for further studies and this opportunity came in 1964. By 1968 when Port Harcourt fell to federal forces during the war, Mr. Felix fled to his hometown with only a briefcase containing a few clothes and his certificates. Life was really tough for him and his wife as they were not prepared for the kind of survival the impact of the war meted out on them. Shortly after the war in 1970, Mr. Felix decided to go back to Port Harcourt and begin a new Life. This was not easy as he could not find employment even with his qualifications as a result of the discriminations and victimization post war. It was not until 1972 that the discrimination eased that he was able to bring his wife over to Port Harcourt and by 1974 he secured employment at the Nigeria Engineering Works (NEW) Limited Port Harcourt as an Accounting Assistant, a position far less than his qualifications which he accepted anyway. This was the beginning of his corporate life as he worked, got further on the job training and rose in ranks until his retirement in 1998 as Assistant Manager Accounts Department after 25 years of meritorious service. During his career he built great friendships with his colleagues that lasted much later after retirement. Mr. Felix had a brief stint with other employments such as Sudelettra, Daewoo Nigeria Limited until his final retirement into private life after the death of his wife in 2004.

Social Life

People who knew Prince Felix Amaefule can attest that he was not a very social person unlike his wife Comfort Amaefule. Together they complemented each other. As a trader, she was amiable and can easily relate with people, Prince Felix, as an accountant, was quieter and more conservative. Nevertheless, he was jovial to his close friends and had a good sense of humor. He built rich friendships with neighbors and co-landlords and tenants in his neighbourhood. His friendship with some brethren continued irrespective of distance. Notable was Bro Edet Akpan who was a true friend to the end.

Community Life

Prince Felix Amaefule was actively involved in community associations such as Amumara Development Union (ADU) Port Harcourt branch as well as Okpanku meetings in Port Harcourt. However, after the demise of his wife in 2004, he retreated to his private life. He was a strong support to his extended family and kinsmen. He supported many in the family, kindred and beyond to secure jobs to advance their means of livelihood. He never discriminated when it comes to supporting people.
